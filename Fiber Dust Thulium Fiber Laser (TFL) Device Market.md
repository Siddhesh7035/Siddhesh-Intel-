A fiber dust thulium fiber laser (TFL) device is a type of laser system that uses a thulium-doped fiber to generate laser light. The fiber is doped with thulium, a rare earth element, which allows the fiber to generate light at a wavelength of approximately 1900 nm, which is within the near-infrared range.

The laser light generated by a TFL device can be used for a variety of applications, such as cutting, welding, marking, and engraving.

Innovations in laser technology, particularly TFL, have enabled safer and more effective procedures in urology, lithotripsy, and soft tissue surgeries. These advancements are boosting adoption in healthcare facilities worldwide.

The growing incidence of kidney stones and other urological conditions has created a demand for efficient treatment options, positioning TFL devices as a preferred choice for healthcare providers.
Increasing Healthcare Expenditure

Rising investments in healthcare infrastructure and technologies, especially in emerging economies, are creating a conducive environment for the adoption of advanced surgical tools like TFL devices.
