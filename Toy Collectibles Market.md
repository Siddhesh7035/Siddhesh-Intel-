Key Trends in the Toy Collectibles Market:
Rise of Pop Culture Collectibles: There is a growing demand for collectible toys based on popular movies, TV shows, video games, and other pop culture franchises like Marvel, Star Wars, Pokemon, etc. These licensed products attract fans and drive sales.

Limited Edition and Exclusives: Toy companies are increasingly releasing limited edition and exclusive variant versions of popular collectible lines to drive demand from avid collectors.

Adult Collector Base: A significant portion of the collector market comprises adults nostalgic for childhood toys or fans of specific franchises/characters. This has led to more collectibles aimed at adult hobbyists.

Vinyl Art Toys: Designer vinyl art toys/figures from brands like Kidrobot, Funko, and others have gained immense popularity as affordable collectible art pieces.

Online Marketplaces: The rise of online platforms like eBay, Facebook groups has facilitated the buying/selling of rare and vintage collectible toys among collectors globally.

Graded Collectibles: Third-party grading and authentication services have emerged to certify the condition and value of high-end collectible toys.

Digital Collectibles: With the growth of NFTs and blockchain, there are new opportunities for digital/virtual toy collectibles as well.
